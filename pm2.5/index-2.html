*what is PM2.5?

PM, short for particulate matter, consists of microscopic particles lgall enough to be suspended in the air for a long period of time. PM2.5 refers to very fine particles whose diameters are less than 2.5 micrometer (µm), which is 0.0025 millimeter or 0.00001 inch. they are often the direct result of man-made activities, such as coal and diesel burning, vehicle emission, construction site, and agriculture waste combustion.

tiny as they are, PM2.5 particles are invisible to our naked eyes. the background of this page is an image of a single human hair magnified 1,000 - 10,000 times depending on your screen size. scroll down to see how PM2.5 compares.

as we can see, PM2.5 particles are even smaller than a red blood cell. because of this, they are capable of traversing our airways and penetrate into our lungs, from which they find their ways into our blood vessels and into other organs, such as the brain, liver, and kidney. as they are made up of chemicals and heavy metals, they are capable of causing inflammation, oxidative stress, blockage, and ultimately cancer.

cloth mask

cloth masks, bandanas, and cloth handkerchiefs are often used in developing countries thanks to their inexpensive price and reusability. they provide a certain degree of protection especially against larger particles; however, they only offer marginal shield against PM2.5 particles. the tighter the fabric is, the better the protection.

surgical mask

disposable surgical masks are affordable, convenient, and moderately effective against fine dust particles. if tightly worn, surgical masks can filter up to 80% particles while also protecting against water droplets that can carry bacteria and viruses.

N95 mask

N95 masks, if worn properly, can filter at least 95% airborne particles. they are commonly utilized by construction workers and healthcare workers for their great efficiency. during the past few years, N95 masks have gained considerable popularity among people in China and India as they face some of the worst air pollutions on the planet.

being indoor

indoor dust particles concentration can vary considerably from household to household. indoor air quality depends on the air tightness of the building and whether it is equipped with air filters. at the same time, air quality can be significantly worsened by indoor emission activities within a confined space, such as smoking, cooking, and dusting.

HEPA filter

HEPA, which stands for high-efficiency particulate air, can successfully filter out 99.95% of airborne particles smaller than 2.5um. made from tightly-packed non-woven fibers, HEPA filters are used in indoor air purifiers and modern HVAC system. it has been estimated that such systems can reduce premature mortality caused by air pollution by up to 2.5%.

methodology and reference

calculating the particles numbers

real-time and historical data are obtained from openAQ[1], an open-source platform that gathers air pollution data from monitoring stations all over the world

the data are compared to the daily limit of 25 µm/m3 set by WHO[2]

the obtained pm2.5 data are then converted from mass concentration (µm/m3) to particle number concentration (particle/m3) using different empirical models from Franken et al.[3]

the converted particle number concentration data are then multiplied by the inhalation rate for an average adult[4] (16 m3 or 565 ft3 per day) to obtain the estimated number of inhaled particles
